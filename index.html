What is Body Mass Index (BMI)?

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a widely used measurement to assess weight status and potential health risks associated with being underweight or overweight. It calculates an individual's body mass based on their weight and height.

Why was BMI developed?

BMI was first introduced in the 19th century by Belgian mathematician Adolphe Quetelet as a way to describe the "average man." It has since become a popular tool for assessing weight status, with various modifications and refinements over time.
